The Product Story

What problem does this solve — and why does it exist?

产品故事

它解决了什么问题——为什么存在？

30 minutes. Every. Single. Job.

Picture the job search grind. You find an interesting role. Then the clock starts — read the JD carefully, rewrite your cover letter for this specific company, figure out which keywords the ATS scanner is looking for, assess whether your experience actually matches the requirements.

That's 30+ minutes. Multiply by 50 applications. That's a part-time job just to apply for a job.

每投一份简历，都要花30分钟

想象求职的日常：找到一个感兴趣的职位，时钟开始倒计时——仔细阅读JD，针对这家公司重写求职信，研究ATS关键词，评估自身经历是否符合要求。

这一套下来要30分钟以上。投50份就是25小时——相当于一份兼职。

30 seconds instead of 30 minutes

The AI Job Application Agent collapses that 30-minute process into a single action. Paste a job URL or description. In about 15–20 seconds, you get everything you need to apply.

从30分钟压缩到30秒

AI 求职助手把这套繁琐流程压缩成一个动作。粘贴职位链接或描述，约15–20秒后，所有申请材料一次性到位。

The PM lens on the core promise

The product's value can be measured by the time it reclaims and the quality it preserves. A good PM wouldn't just say "make it faster" — they'd define what faster means and how to know if they got there.

产品经理视角：核心承诺

产品价值体现在两个维度：节省的时间，以及保留的质量。优秀的产品经理不会只说"做得更快"，而是会明确"快"的定义，以及如何验证是否达到目标。

One person. One profile. A deliberate choice.

The entire product is designed for a single user. Your profile lives in one file. There's no login, no accounts, no way to share. This isn't an oversight — it's a deliberate scope decision.

单人使用，一个档案——刻意为之

整个产品为单个用户设计。你的档案存在一个文件里，没有登录、没有账户、无法多人共享。这不是疏漏——而是经过深思熟虑的范围决策。

The Metrics

How would you know if this product is actually working?

核心指标

你怎么知道这个产品是否真的在发挥作用？

Speed is easy to measure. Quality is hard.

Think of a car dashboard. You don't stare at the speedometer constantly — but you'd notice immediately if it disappeared. Metrics work the same way. For this product, the obvious metric is speed — but speed alone is a trap. A product that generates instant, generic cover letters is fast but worthless. The real challenge is measuring quality at speed.

速度容易衡量，质量很难

想象一辆汽车的仪表盘。你不会一直盯着速度计——但如果它消失了，你会立刻察觉。指标就是这样运作的。对这款产品而言，最直观的指标是速度——但速度本身是个陷阱。一个能即时生成通用求职信的产品很快，却毫无价值。真正的挑战是衡量兼顾质量的速度。

📋 If This Were a PRD 如果这是一份 PRD
The success metrics section might read: "Primary: time-to-application-ready per job (target: under 60 seconds). Quality guardrail: cover letter must reference the specific company and role — generic outputs fail. Honesty guardrail: match score must surface real gaps, not inflate fit."
PRD的成功指标部分可能写道："主要指标：每个职位的申请准备时间（目标：60秒以内）。质量护栏：求职信必须提及具体公司和职位名称，通用输出视为不达标。诚实护栏：匹配分数必须真实反映差距，不得虚高。"

The product already measures itself

One of this product's most interesting design choices: it explicitly commits to honest match scoring. The README cites a 28% score for a trading analyst role as a feature, not a bug — it correctly flagged missing domain knowledge. A product that inflates scores feels good in the moment but destroys trust when users apply to mismatched roles and get rejected.

产品已经在自我衡量

这款产品最有趣的设计选择之一：它明确承诺诚实的匹配评分。README 将一次交易分析师职位28%的分数作为产品亮点——因为它准确地揭示了候选人缺乏的领域知识。虚高分数短期感觉良好，但当用户因此投递不匹配职位而被拒时，信任就被摧毁了。

✅

A trading role requiring FX knowledge correctly scored low for a candidate without that background.

诚实是产品特性

Every product decision is a seesaw

Product decisions are made under real constraints — limited time, real API costs, a single developer, specific use cases in mind. Every choice pushes one side of the seesaw up and the other down. A PM's job isn't to judge these choices — it's to understand what they reveal about priorities.

每个产品决策都是一个跷跷板

产品决策在真实约束下做出——时间有限、API成本真实存在、只有一个开发者、心里有特定的使用场景。每个选择都让跷跷板一端上升，另一端下降。产品经理的工作不是评判这些选择的对错——而是理解它们揭示了什么优先级。

AI 如何创造产品的核心价值——是什么让它难以复制？

AI as a scalpel, not a Swiss Army knife

Many AI products use AI for everything — storing data, fetching URLs, sending messages. This creates fragile systems where AI's unpredictability bleeds into every layer. This product takes a different approach: AI is used for exactly one job — reasoning. Everything else is handled by deterministic tools. The README: "If the system still works after replacing Claude with another model, then you've built a true AI agent."

AI 是手术刀，不是万能工具

很多 AI 产品把 AI 用于一切——存储数据、抓取网页、发送消息。这造就了脆弱的系统，AI 的不确定性渗透每一层。这款产品采取了不同做法：AI 只做一件事——推理。其他一切由确定性工具处理。README中说："如果用另一个大模型替换 Claude 后系统仍能运行，那你才真正构建了一个 AI 智能体。"

🤖
AI does: Reasoning
Job-to-profile analysis, skill gap assessment, cover letter generation, interview coaching. Tasks requiring judgment and context.
AI 负责：推理
职位与档案分析、技能差距评估、求职信生成、面试辅导。需要判断力和上下文理解的任务。

Tools do: Everything else
Fetching URLs, loading YAML, saving JSON, exporting files, syncing to Feishu. Deterministic, testable, AI-free. The AI never touches I/O or storage.

The reliability problem AI products all face

AI models are powerful but unpredictable. Ask them to return structured data and they might add a preamble, change format, or wrap JSON in a code block. This product solves it with delimiter-based output parsing — the AI is instructed to wrap outputs in exact markers. The product extracts sections by looking for those markers.

所有 AI 产品都面临的可靠性问题

大语言模型强大但不可预测。要求返回结构化数据时，它可能添加前言、改变格式或把JSON放在代码块里。这款产品用分隔符解析来解决——指示 AI 用精确标记包裹输出，产品通过查找这些标记来提取内容。

🏷️
Delimiter markers
The AI wraps each section in exact markers. The product extracts content between markers — guaranteed clean parsing regardless of content length or style.
分隔符标记
AI 用精确标记包裹每个部分。产品提取标记之间的内容——无论内容长度或风格如何，解析都有保障。
🔁
Dual-format output
Every analysis returns Markdown (human reading) + JSON (machine processing). One AI call produces both.
双格式输出
每次分析同时返回 Markdown（人类阅读）+ JSON（机器处理）。一次 AI 调用产出两种格式。
📋 If This Were a PRD 如果这是一份 PRD
The AI reliability requirement: "The system must successfully parse Claude's output 100% of the time. Any response that fails to parse must trigger a user-facing retry message, not a silent failure."
AI 可靠性要求："系统必须100%成功解析 Claude 的输出。任何解析失败的响应必须触发面向用户的重试提示，而不是静默失败。" 分隔符技术正是满足这一标准的实现方案。

Every gap is a product bet waiting to be made

What a product doesn't do is just as telling as what it does. Gaps reveal where the team drew a line — out of time, because the use case wasn't their own, or because the complexity wasn't worth it yet. For a PM, gaps aren't failures — they're a roadmap in disguise.

每个差距都是一个待做的产品押注

产品没有做的事情和它做了的事情同样能说明问题。差距揭示了团队在哪里划定了边界——因为时间不够、因为这个场景不是他们自己的、或者因为当时的复杂度还不值得。对产品经理来说，差距不是失败——而是乔装成问题的路线图。
